## 1. Topological foundations of DNA as an electromagnetic inductor

The physical transformation of DNA from a relaxed B-form double helix into a supercoiled plectonemic state is a fundamental mechanical response to the torsional stresses inherent in cellular processes such as transcription and replication. The physics of this transition is governed by the interconversion of **twist (Tw)** and **writhe (Wr)** as described by the topological **link number (Lk)**. In an electromagnetic context, the double helix represents a primary winding, while the plectonemic supercoil represents a secondary, higher-order winding.
