Real-data default. Dynamic layers use only remote APIs (NOAA SWPC, Open-Meteo soil, OpenWebRX when configured, HIFLD, geomagnetic). There is no synthetic soil fill and no fabricated spatial sin-phase on gates. With TURNER_SDR_WSS_URL, per-gate strengths follow real downstream RMS slices from your OpenWebRX buffer; otherwise coupling is uniform from live Kp / IQ RMS (unless legacy TURNER_ALLOW_SYNTHETIC=1). Passive fence read: configure a receive-only OpenWebRX wss://…/ws/ on the server so ingest pulls live FFT/IQ/demod floats, chunked into RMS along the passband and mapped to ordered perimeter gates (spectral proxy — not a discrete ranch transducer per gate). Herd dots are still a weighted field over pastures (inverse-style placement), not GPS or scale weights. Registry numbers (head count, ADM, etc.) are static published baselines in data/turner-public-registry.json, not live measurements. Date range: requires Open-Meteo soil for every geolocated pasture on every day in the window, or the API returns an error (set TURNER_ALLOW_SYNTHETIC=1 on the server to restore legacy synthetic fallback and φ-squashed Kp / RNG weight spread). Triangulated lock-in (default on): Fence pulse (wirePhaseUs PLL from OpenWebRX + Kp) × SDR (per-gate spectrum when TURNER_SDR_WSS_URL is set) × satellite surface (Open-Meteo soil + NASA POWER skin temp IR proxy) × fence lines from OpenStreetMap (Overpass barrier=fence near each pasture — no field walk required; ODbL, often incomplete on private ranch) with optional local override in data/turner-perimeter-steel.geojson × Digital Pru φ-gate. Raises lockIn and biases the placement field toward mapped fence — still a model, not collar or scale truth. Optional ET₀ calibration: TURNER_FUSION_SURFACE_CALIB=1 adds a small bounded pseudo-correlation nudge from Open-Meteo FAO-56 ET₀. On-the-ground collaboration: accuracy and operational usefulness can be significantly improved by tuning this stack with Turner teams in the field — pasture boundaries, fence and gate truth where OSM is thin, OpenWebRX / hydrogen-line receiver placement, seasonal baselines, and which components to weight for each ranch — without requiring a full re-walk when public fence data or prior surveys already exist.
